Dr. Nasr Abu Zayd

Egyptian thinker, author, academic and one of the leading liberal theologians in Islam

Nasr Hamid Abu Zayd during the Ibn Rushd Prize awarding ceremony 2005

Here's a time line of Dr. Nasr Abu Zayd's life:

  • 1943 -Born in Quhafa, Egypt
  • 1972 -BA degree in Arabic Studies
  • 1977 -MA degrees in Islamic Studies
  • 1981 -PhD degrees in Islamic Studies
  • 1982 - Asistant professor at Cairo University
  • 1985 -Visiting lecturer at Osaka University in Japan
  • 1992 - Sued by conservative Muslims and a Court of Appeals declared him an apostate and divorced him from his wife
  • 1995 -Exile in Netherlands and Visiting Professor at the Leiden University
  • 2010 -died at the age of 66
The prize was announced to be awarded to an Arab scholar of Islam who strives for a fundamental reform of Islamic thought, and who works towards a reapprochement between Islamic tradition and modernity.
